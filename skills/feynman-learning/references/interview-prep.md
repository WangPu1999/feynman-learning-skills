# Graduate Interview Preparation

Use for master's students preparing for jobs, internships, research internships,
PhD-style interviews, or industry roles. The goal is not memorized answers. The
goal is to turn academic and project experience into clear, defensible interview
answers.

## Interview Surfaces

Pick the relevant surface instead of drilling everything:

- **Research story**: thesis, paper, lab project, research question, method,
  result, limitation, and future work.
- **Project story**: goal, system design, data, implementation, tradeoffs,
  debugging, evaluation, and impact.
- **Technical fundamentals**: core concepts, algorithms, math/statistics, ML,
  engineering basics, or field-specific foundations.
- **Paper/method discussion**: baseline, design logic, why it works, weakness,
  and possible improvement.
- **Role fit**: why this role, why this team, what strengths transfer from the
  learner's research or projects.
- **Behavioral evidence**: collaboration, failure, ambiguity, ownership,
  communication, and learning speed.
- **English interview**: concise academic/professional expression, paraphrase,
  and follow-up handling.

## Technical Concept Fallback

Technical interviews often expose concepts the learner has not met or cannot
yet explain. Do not force a polished interview answer when the concept is fuzzy.

Use this route:

```text
unfamiliar concept -> Feynman loop -> boundary check if technical
-> concise interview answer -> likely follow-ups
```

Ask:

```text
你能不能先不用术语解释这个概念？
如果不能，我们先回到费曼循环，把它讲清楚，再转成面试答案。
```

Only return to the interview frame after the learner can explain:

- what it is
- why it matters
- how it works
- one concrete example
- one boundary, tradeoff, or common follow-up

## Answer Shape

Use the smallest shape that fits:

```text
Claim -> Evidence -> Tradeoff -> Reflection
```

For research/project answers:

```text
Problem -> Why It Matters -> Method -> My Contribution -> Evidence -> Limitation -> Next Step
```

For behavioral answers:

```text
Situation -> Task -> Action -> Result -> Reflection
```

## Graduate-Level Follow-Up Lens

Ask:

- What exactly was your contribution?
- Why did you choose this method instead of the baseline?
- What assumptions does your work rely on?
- What failed or did not work?
- How did you evaluate it?
- What would you change with more time or data?
- How does this connect to the target role?
- What technical concept might the interviewer ask next?
